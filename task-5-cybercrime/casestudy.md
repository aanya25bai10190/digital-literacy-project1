# Case Study: OTP Fraud via Fake Customer Support

## Overview
One-Time Password fraud is a type of attack where scammers trick victims into revealing OTPs sent by their bank or payment apps.

## Target Audience
- **Online shoppers** who frequently shop online and make digital transactions.  
- **Elderly or less tech-savvy users** who may be unfamiliar with OTP security.  
- **Students and young adults** who use UPI, mobile banking, or digital wallets frequently.

## Case Example
A user named Shreya receives a call from someone claiming to be a representative of her bank. The caller said there was some kind of “suspicious activity” on her account and that they needed to verify her identity immediately.

The scammer asked Shreya to provide the OTP she has received on her phone, assuring her that it was for verification. Believing the call to be genuine, Priya shared the OTP.

Immediately after, ₹25,000 were withdrawn from her bank account. The scammer had used the OTP to authorize a fraudulent transaction.

## How the Fraud Works
- Fraudsters impersonate bank or payment app officials.
- They create the urgency and fear to manipulate victims.
- OTPs are **always for authorizing transactions**; banks never ask for them over phone calls or messages.

## Consequences
- **Financial loss:** Direct withdrawal from bank accounts and loss of hard-earned money.
- **Emotional distress:** Anxiety and fear because one has been cheated.
- **Recovery difficulty:** Once the OTP is used, transactions cannot usually be reversed.

## Key Takeaways
1. Never share OTPs with anyone, even if they claim to be bank officials.
2. Banks or payment apps will **never ever ask for OTPs over calls or messages**.
3. Report suspicious calls to your bank immediately.
