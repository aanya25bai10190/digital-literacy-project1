# Case Study: Ransomware Attack on a Personal Laptop

## Overview  
Ransomware is a type of malicious software that blocks access to a system or files by encrypting them. Attackers then demand a ransom payment in exchange for restoring access.

## Target Audience  
- **Students and professionals** who store important files on personal devices  
- **Small business owners** with limited cybersecurity measures  
- **General internet users** who download files or click on unknown links  

## Case Example  
A college student named **Shrey** receives an email that appears to be from a job portal, offering internship opportunities. The email contains an attachment labeled “Internship_Details.pdf.exe”.

Assuming it to be a PDF file, Shrey downloads and opens it. Nothing appears to happen initially, so he ignores it.

A few hours later, Shrey notices that all his files—documents, photos, and projects—are no longer accessible. Each file has been renamed, and a message appears on his screen stating:

> "Your files have been encrypted. Pay ₹50,000 in cryptocurrency within 72 hours to recover your data."

Shrey realizes that his system has been infected with ransomware. Without backups, he is unable to recover his important academic files.

## How the Attack Works  
- Attackers send malicious files via email, downloads, or fake websites  
- The victim unknowingly installs the ransomware  
- The malware encrypts files and locks access  
- A ransom note is displayed demanding payment for decryption  

## Consequences  
- **Data loss:** Important files become inaccessible  
- **Financial loss:** Victims may feel pressured to pay the ransom  
- **Productivity impact:** Work, studies, or business operations are disrupted  
- **No guarantee of recovery:** Even after payment, files may not be restored  

## Key Takeaways  
1. Never download or open attachments from unknown or suspicious sources  
2. Always check file extensions (e.g., `.exe` vs `.pdf`)  
3. Regularly back up important data to external drives or cloud storage  
4. Use updated antivirus and security software  
5. Avoid clicking on suspicious links or pop-ups  
6. Keep your system and software updated with the latest security patches  
7. Do not pay the ransom—report the incident to cybersecurity authorities instead
